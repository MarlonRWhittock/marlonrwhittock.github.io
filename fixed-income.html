U.S. Fixed Income & Credit
Market Intelligence

Fixed income markets are the backbone of the global financial system and one of the most powerful leading indicators of economic direction. Treasury yields reflect the market's collective expectations for growth, inflation, and Federal Reserve policy, while corporate credit spreads reveal how investors are pricing risk across the economy. When the yield curve steepens, it signals economic acceleration ahead. When it inverts, it has historically preceded every U.S. recession. Credit spreads tightening means confidence is building; widening means stress is forming beneath the surface. This research platform tracks these dynamics in real time, translating bond market signals into actionable insights for GDP forecasting, sector rotation, and portfolio positioning. Whether you're managing a multi-asset portfolio or building conviction around a macro trade, understanding the fixed income landscape gives you a forward-looking edge that equity-only analysis simply cannot provide.

Methodology
Analytical framework & data sources

This research analyzes the U.S. Treasury yield curve and corporate credit markets to identify rate regime shifts, credit stress signals, and their transmission into equity sector performance. The yield curve framework decomposes movements into level, slope, and curvature factors across the full maturity spectrum â from the Fed Funds rate through the 30-year bond. Credit analysis layers in investment-grade and high-yield corporate spreads to gauge risk appetite and default expectations. Rate cut expectations are derived from Treasury curve spreads versus the Fed Funds rate using an expectations hypothesis model. Equity sector implications are determined by mapping rate and credit signals through historically validated transmission channels, cross-referenced across multiple rate regimes since 1990.

◆ Data Sources

Treasury yields sourced from the Federal Reserve H.15 release (daily constant-maturity rates). Corporate bond yields from ICE BofAML indices via FRED (AAA, BBB, CCC & Below). ETF prices from market close data. All series updated weekly with daily granularity where available. Historical depth: 60+ years for Treasuries, 30 years for corporate indices.

◆ Yield Curve Framework

Yield curve analysis decomposes movements into level, slope, and curvature factors. Key spreads tracked: 2s10s (cycle signal), Fed Fundsâ2yr (policy expectations), 10yrâ30yr (term premium). Curve shape classified as: inverted, flat, normal, or steep â with bull/bear qualifiers based on rate direction.

◆ Rate Expectations Model

Implied rate paths derived from Treasury curve spreads vs. Fed Funds rate. Each maturity's spread is translated into implied 25bp moves using a simple expectations hypothesis framework. Scenario probabilities estimated from curve-implied terminal rates matched against current 1yr and 2yr yields. Weekly trend captures shifts in market pricing.

◆ Equity Transmission

Rate and credit signals are mapped to equity sectors through established transmission channels: discount rate effects (growth/tech), NIM sensitivity (banks/insurance), credit availability (REITs/utilities), and commodity-rate correlations (energy/materials). Historical correlations validated across multiple rate regimes since 1990.
